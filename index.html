№ 02  ·  The mental load

It almost always falls on one person.

If you're the parent who knows when the school holiday starts, what your in-laws are doing this weekend, when your child's passport expires, which suitcase is in the loft, and what's defrosting in the fridge — you're carrying the mental load of a modern family.

It's most often a mother who carries it — but in plenty of homes it's simply whoever runs the logistics: the parent who travels less, the single earner, the one who quietly became the family's organiser. Whoever it is in your home, the weight is real — and it shouldn't sit on one set of shoulders.

Vesta exists to take that load off your head — and put it somewhere your whole family can share it.

№ 03  ·  What makes Vesta different

Four ideas that shape every screen.

01.

One person, many households.

One login holds every household you belong to — your home, your parents' place abroad, a co-parent's home — so there's no separate app or group chat for each side of the family.

Most family apps assume your family lives under one roof. Real life is messier. You have a current household. You may also have parents and siblings in another country, a co-parent in another city, kids growing up between two homes, or in-laws who want to be closer to the grandkids.

Vesta is built around multi-household identity from day one — one account that spans every household you belong to, with a "My Day" view that pulls together everything that matters across all of them.

This is not a feature. It is the foundation.

02.

Your family, in your language.

Everyone sees the same family life in their own language and timezone — so no one's left out by a language or a clock that isn't theirs.

Each family member sees Vesta in their own language, timezone, date format, and currency — automatically, per person. The app, the calendar, your AI-generated daily brief, and notifications all adapt to whoever's looking.

A school event imported in Dutch surfaces in your daily brief in your preferred language; a reminder for a relative in another country arrives in their timezone, not yours. Multilingual families are the norm for a Vesta family — we treat them that way.

03.

AI that does the thinking.

Stop typing life into forms: snap a flyer, forward an email, or just say it — and the right calendars and to-do lists fill themselves in.

Forget filling out forms. Snap a photo of a school flyer, forward an email confirmation, or speak into the mic — Vesta extracts the events, tasks, and deadlines and slots them into the right calendars and to-do lists.

The AI does more than capture: it surfaces what matters.

"Wednesday is stacked — Lily's swimming lesson, the weekly shop, and your usual catch-up with your parents. Want me to move the shop to Saturday morning so the evening feels less rushed?"

Outcome-focused, not output-focused. Every AI suggestion is measurable, and every correction makes the app smarter for your family specifically.

04.

A quiet, helpful partner.

Help arrives when it actually counts and stays quiet when it doesn't — none of the badge-spam and notification noise other apps bury you in.

No notification spam. No "3 tasks due" badges. Vesta is designed to feel like a calm presence in your day: outcome-focused nudges when they help, silence when they don't, and the kind of small daily wins that make a busy family feel less chaotic.

№ 07  ·  Questions

Questions, answered honestly.

What is Vesta?

A family management app — calendar, tasks, lists, photos, budget, and household chat — built around an AI that takes the mental load off the parent who's been carrying it. Mobile-first.

Who is it for?

Modern families with multi-country, multilingual, or multi-household lives. If your family lives across countries, languages, or homes — Vesta is for you, expat or not.

When will it launch?

We're currently in build, with private beta opening to early waitlist members in 2026. Public launch follows once we're confident the product holds up for real families.

How is it different from Apple Family Sharing or Google Family Link?

Those tools assume every family member shares the same home country. Vesta is built for families that live across countries, languages, and timezones from day one — and adds a real coordination layer on top: tasks, photos, household budget, and chat in one place.

How is it different from other family apps?

Vesta is European-built, multi-household by design (one account spans every household you belong to), multilingual per family member, AI-native, and GDPR-by-design. That combination is what makes Vesta the right fit for cross-border, multilingual, modern families.

Will it be available in my language?

Vesta launches in English, with Dutch following shortly after. German, French, and Turkish are next on the roadmap. Tell us your preferred language when you join the waitlist — we use that to prioritise.

Will my family's data be safe?

Yes. Vesta is GDPR-native, with EU data residency, no third-party tracking, and no selling of your data. Your household's data is scoped to your household, and you can export or delete it at any time.

What about elderly relatives who aren't tech-savvy?

Vesta is being designed so that a grandparent on a basic smartphone in another country can participate without an account-setup ordeal. Receiving photos, seeing a shared calendar, joining a family chat — that should be effortless on Day 1.

How much will it cost?

Vesta will have a free tier covering the essential coordination features for every family. Premium features will be priced fairly in EUR — we'll publish the details closer to launch. Waitlist members get extended free access to premium features during beta.

Are you hiring, fundraising, or open to partnerships?

We're independent and intend to stay that way. If you're an international school, an expat-community publisher, an HR mobility lead at a NL employer, or a relocation specialist — and you'd like to be involved in the beta — drop us a line at hello@vesta.social.
